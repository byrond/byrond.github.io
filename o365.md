Yesterday, I got the notification that I had only 25% left of my free 5GB iCloud storage, so I started shopping around.
I had been wanting to move my Google Photos anyway and hadn't settled on an alternate solution. Apple's offering is
reasonable, topping out at $9.99 per month for 2TB. That's on par with Google, DropBox and Sync.com (which is slightly cheaper
at $8 for 2TB). Then, I looked into Microsoft's OneDrive.

For the same price, I could get Office 365 Home, which includes up to 6 users each having 1TB available (I'm guessing I
could create multiple accounts for myself if I needed the space). They also have a Personal plan for $6.99, which amounts
to a single-user version of Home. Out of curiosity, I looked at the business plans, and my inner nerd was awakened.

Office 365 Business Essentials (or Basic if you're reading this after April 20, 2020) is $5 per user per month, and yes, you
can buy a single business user license. It includes Exchange, OneDrive, SharePoint, and Teams. It does not include desktop
versions of Office, but you can still use the web and mobile apps. Signing up before June 30, 2020 will get you 6 months free.

This isn't a consumer product and requires some admin chops. Luckily, I have been Novell, Windows, and Unix administrators
in the past, so it couldn't be that hard. Right? It's been awhile since I've done any real admin work, and I've never been
an O365 admin, but it actually wasn't that difficult. They have good guided setup documentation, and it was fairly easy to
get my domain connected, email working, and my one user setup with OneDrive.

Now, the fun begins...
